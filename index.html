Measure Production Style, Not Content Quality

This project measures production-intensity patterns found in children's media, using a research taxonomy of pacing, audio intensity, and reward patterning. It does not judge content appropriateness, educational value, or safety, and it makes no claim that any score causes harm.

How to read these scores

Every video gets an overall score and a breakdown across three categories: Pacing Intensification (how fast the video moves visually), Recovery Denial (how rarely it lets a viewer calm back down), and Reward Patterning (how often it sets up a small payoff).

  • Good calmer than most of the dataset
  • Moderate typical for this dataset
  • Extremely High substantially more intense than most

Scores are relative to this dataset sample, not an absolute or medical scale. This tool measures production style only. It makes no claim about developmental harm.
